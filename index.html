The Film

A day on the mountain

"They're always watching. You just don't see them."

On Guard follows a full shift with the volunteer ski patrol at Labrador Mountain — from the quiet of early morning before the lifts turn, to the final sweep of an empty hill at night.

The film reveals the unseen systems that make skiing in the Syracuse mountains possible, and puts a human face on the people who run them without recognition, without applause, and without an audience.

This is not a story about drama. It is a story about discipline, awareness, and the satisfaction of work that goes unnoticed — because when it's done right, there is nothing to notice.

Morning brief & gear check

Patrol skis every run top to bottom before the lifts open. They're looking for overnight hazards — ice, debris, boundary issues. First tracks on the mountain belong to them.

Crowd presence & operations
The mountain fills with kids, families, weekend skiers. Amid the noise, patrol is constantly watching from above — aware of everyone, visible to almost no one. Radios stay busy.

Incident response
